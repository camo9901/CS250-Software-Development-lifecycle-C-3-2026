# CS250-Software-Development-lifecycle-C-3-2026

Sprint Review and Retrospective

During this course, I learned how Agile and Scrum support software development by helping teams collaborate, adapt to changing requirements, and continuously improve throughout the development process. Working through the SNHU Travel project gave me experience creating user stories, prioritizing a product backlog, writing test cases, and understanding the responsibilities of different Scrum roles. My favorite part of the course was working as a developer because I enjoyed creating user stories and thinking about how customer requirements could be translated into software features.

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?:

I learned that understanding user needs starts by thinking about what the customer is trying to accomplish instead of immediately focusing on the technical solution. User stories helped me break requirements into smaller, manageable tasks that described who the user is, what they need, and why it is valuable. This made it easier to prioritize work and build features that focused on delivering value instead of simply completing technical tasks.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?:

I approach development by breaking large projects into smaller pieces that can be completed one step at a time. This course showed me the value of planning work through a product backlog, estimating effort using story points, and continuously reviewing progress throughout each sprint. In future projects, I want to continue using user stories, sprint planning, regular feedback, and iterative development because these practices make it easier to adapt when requirements change and help keep projects organized.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?:

To me, being a good team member means communicating clearly, being willing to collaborate, and taking responsibility for my work. Throughout this course, I learned that software development is not only about writing code. Successful projects depend on teamwork, sharing ideas, helping teammates solve problems, and being open to feedback. I also learned that understanding different Scrum roles helps everyone work together more effectively toward a common goal.
